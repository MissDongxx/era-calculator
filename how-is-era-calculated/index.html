How Is ERA Calculated

ERA is calculated as a rate stat: earned runs are scaled to a full official game and then divided by innings pitched.

Direct answer

The calculation is earned runs multiplied by game length, divided by innings pitched. A lower result means the pitcher allows fewer earned runs per full game.

Why innings matter

Innings pitched make the stat comparable. Without innings, a pitcher who allowed 2 earned runs in 2 innings would look the same as a pitcher who allowed 2 earned runs in 8 innings.

Partial outs

One extra out is one-third of an inning. Two extra outs are two-thirds of an inning. That conversion should happen before the final calculation.
